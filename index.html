How This ‘New-Aged Device’ Got My Puppy to Finally Behave

I thought I’d have to find a new home for my Boxer...
thankfully, I discovered BarxBuddy.

Eric Murphy 31 March 2021

Does your puppy misbehave?

Do they bark excessively, jump, dig, or chew on things they shouldn’t, even after plenty of exercise?

If so, I know how stressful it can be.

Elmo, my Boxer rescue, was the most handsome dog I’d ever seen.

But he was a total nightmare as a puppy.

I adopted Elmo at 7 months, the age where Boxers start to “test the waters” and assert their dominance. For Elmo, that meant chewing. Lots and lots of chewing.

At first, he would just grab an old tennis shoe and run off with it. I actually thought it was kind of cute… until things started to escalate.

Soon, Elmo wasn’t just grabbing sneakers... he was gnawing on anything and everything he could find: furniture, books, remote controls. You name it, he would chew it to pieces.

I tried everything I knew to stop it. I took him out for long walks every day, and bought him about 20 different chew toys.

I even got a spray bottle and would spritz Elmo with water and say “No!” when I caught him misbehaving.

It worked at first… but after about 5 times, he got used to it, and ignored the spray.

Eventually, I resorted to putting “Bitter Apple” on the legs of all my furniture and on my shoes. That didn’t work either.

Then things finally came to a head.

One night I was cooking dinner, listening to the radio when I heard a loud “crunch, crunch” sound. I looked over, and Elmo had my very expensive iPhone in his mouth, gnawing away.

I screamed at the top of my lungs, “Noo!!! Bad dog, bad dog!” and sprinted towards him. But instead of running away, Elmo didn’t budge.

He just sat there on the floor, iPhone between his paws and he actually snarled at me.

I couldn’t take it, and started bawling my eyes out... I felt angry, ashamed, and defeated.

My beautiful puppy was ruining my home.

And as much as I loved Elmo, something had to change. If it didn’t, I wasn’t sure I could care for him any longer.

Desperate, I called the one person I thought could help.

Advice From an Expert

Jenny is a kind older woman who lives down the street from me.

Besides making the best potato salad in the neighborhood, she’s a dog trainer well-known for raising German Shepherds.

I called her up, and told her what happened.

“Oh boy,” Jenny said with a chuckle. “I think I have just the thing... bring Elmo by tomorrow, and I’ll show you what I know.”

The next morning was a clear, beautiful Saturday. I put Elmo’s leash on, and we walked the four blocks to Jenny’s house.

When we got inside, I told her everything: the non-stop chewing, the destruction, the scary show of aggression. Just telling her about it brought the anxiety back, and I started getting upset again.

What Jenny did next surprised me.

After listening calmly, she got up from her chair and walked into her bedroom. When she came back, she had an old sneaker in her hand.

“Let’s do a little test, shall we?” she said, and set the sneaker in front of Elmo.

“No!”she said in a loud, firm tone.

Unsure what to do, Elmo licked his chops and stared at the shoe. After a few long seconds, he couldn’t hold back and lunged at the sneaker.

Then Jenny did something amazing: she pulled a small device from her pocket, pointed it at Elmo, and pressed a button...

Immediately, he dropped the sneaker and backed away. No growling, no aggression.

My jaw dropped open. “How on earth did you do that?” I asked.

“For headstrong puppies, this is my secret weapon,” she said, holding up the device.

It’s called a BarxBuddy... it uses soundwaves only dogs can hear to break their attention. Just point, press, and the bad behavior stops, instantly.

Jenny said it was the best training tool she had ever found, and was the same one she used with her German Shepherds.

While we talked, she picked the shoe back up and put it in front of Elmo. “No!” she said with the same firm tone.

This time, poor Elmo wouldn’t even look at the sneaker! He turned his head away, and laid down on his side.

I was ecstatic… I felt like Jenny’s ‘secret weapon’ was just the thing I’d been looking for.
